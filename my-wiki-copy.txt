<h2 id="mp-tfa-h2" style="margin:3px; background:#cef2e0; font-family:inherit; font-size:120%; font-weight:bold; border:1px solid #a3bfb1; text-align:left; color:#000; padding:0.2em 0.4em;"><span class="mw-headline" id="From_today.27s_featured_article">From today's featured article</span></h2>

<td style="color:#000;">
<div id="mp-tfa" style="padding:2px 5px">
<div id="mp-tfa-img" style="float: left; margin: 0.5em 0.9em 0.4em 0em;">
<div class="thumbinner mp-thumb" style="background: transparent; border: none; padding: 0; max-width: 120px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Lafayette_dollar_obverse.jpg" class="image" title="Lafayette dollar, obverse"><img alt="Lafayette dollar, obverse" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ed/Lafayette_dollar_obverse.jpg/120px-Lafayette_dollar_obverse.jpg" width="120" height="120" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ed/Lafayette_dollar_obverse.jpg/180px-Lafayette_dollar_obverse.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ed/Lafayette_dollar_obverse.jpg/240px-Lafayette_dollar_obverse.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1509" data-file-height="1512" /></a></div>
</div>
<p>The <b><a href="/wiki/Lafayette_dollar" title="Lafayette dollar">Lafayette dollar</a></b> was an American silver coin issued to raise money for a statue of <a href="/wiki/Gilbert_du_Motier,_Marquis_de_Lafayette" title="Gilbert du Motier, Marquis de Lafayette">Lafayette</a>, the French hero of the <a href="/wiki/American_Revolution" title="American Revolution">American Revolution</a>. The statue was dedicated at the <a href="/wiki/Exposition_Universelle_(1900)" title="Exposition Universelle (1900)">Paris World's Fair of 1900</a>. President <a href="/wiki/William_McKinley" title="William McKinley">William McKinley</a> chose Chicago businessman <a href="/wiki/Ferdinand_Peck" title="Ferdinand Peck">Ferdinand Peck</a> as commissioner-general for the American exhibit at the Paris exposition, and Peck approved the monument for the exhibit. The coin was designed by the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Mint" title="United States Mint">Mint's</a> Chief Engraver, <a href="/wiki/Charles_E._Barber" title="Charles E. Barber">Charles E. Barber</a>, and features conjoined busts of <a href="/wiki/George_Washington" title="George Washington">George Washington</a> and Lafayette on the <a href="/wiki/Obverse_and_reverse" title="Obverse and reverse">obverse</a>. The images are based on a sculpture of Washington by <a href="/wiki/Jean-Antoine_Houdon" title="Jean-Antoine Houdon">Jean-Antoine Houdon</a> and a medal of Lafayette by François-Augustin Caunois. The reverse depicts an early sketch of the planned monument. All the coins were struck at the Philadelphia Mint on December 14, 1899, the centennial of Washington's death. They did not sell out, and 14,000 were later melted by the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Treasury" title="United States Treasury" class="mw-redirect">Treasury</a>. The coins are valued from several hundred dollars to tens of thousands, depending on condition. The Lafayette dollar was the only <a href="/wiki/Dollar_coin_(United_States)" title="Dollar coin (United States)">U.S. silver dollar</a> commemorative prior to 1983, and the first U.S. coin to depict an American citizen. (<a href="/wiki/Lafayette_dollar" title="Lafayette dollar"><b>Full&#160;article...</b></a>)</p>

<p><b><a href="/wiki/David_Villa" title="David Villa">David Villa</a></b> (b. 1981) is a Spanish professional <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a> who plays as a <a href="/wiki/Forward_(association_football)#Striker" title="Forward (association football)">striker</a> and also serves as <a href="/wiki/Captain_(association_football)" title="Captain (association football)">captain</a> for American club <a href="/wiki/New_York_City_FC" title="New York City FC">New York City FC</a>. Before transferring to the United States, Villa played for a number of Spanish teams, including <a href="/wiki/FC_Barcelona" title="FC Barcelona">FC Barcelona</a>, with whom he won his first <a href="/wiki/La_Liga" title="La Liga">La Liga</a> and <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Champions_League" title="UEFA Champions League">UEFA Champions League</a> titles, <a href="/wiki/Valencia_CF" title="Valencia CF">Valencia CF</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Atl%C3%A9tico_de_Madrid" title="Atlético de Madrid" class="mw-redirect">Atlético de Madrid</a>, shown here after scoring a goal. As part of the <a href="/wiki/Spain_national_football_team" title="Spain national football team">Spanish national football team</a>, Villa scored three goals at the <a href="/wiki/2006_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2006 FIFA World Cup">2006 World Cup</a>, was the top scorer at <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Euro_2008" title="UEFA Euro 2008">Euro 2008</a>, and earned the <a href="/wiki/World_Cup_Golden_Boot" title="World Cup Golden Boot" class="mw-redirect">Silver Boot</a> at the <a href="/wiki/2010_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2010 FIFA World Cup">2010 World Cup</a>.</p>
